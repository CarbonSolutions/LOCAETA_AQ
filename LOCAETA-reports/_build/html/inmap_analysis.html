The figures below show the changes in air quality and premature mortality by the CCS technology applied over the LA state. It shows the original emission scenario as well as the modified emission scenario where the NH3 emissions increase is excluded. As expected from the emissions analysis, the amine-based CCS technology increases NH3 gas concentration and NH4 portion of PM2.5, while the modified scenario shows air quality benefits for all species.

The figures below shows the spatial distributions of the PM2.5 changes by the emission scenarios. The NH3 and NH4 increases are particularly high near some of the point-source facilities, not all facility. This leads to both the most postivie and the most negative benefits appeared in the LA state. Excluding NH3 emissions increase, the LA state shows the largest benefits.
